Moderate Dementia

Moderate dementia in Alzheimer's disease is a stage of the disease where cognitive impairment becomes more noticeable and affects daily functioning.


Here are some strategies that can help improve quality of life for individuals with moderate dementia in Alzheimer's disease and their caregivers:

  • Medications: Cholinesterase inhibitors and memantine can help manage symptoms such as memory loss and confusion.
  • Behavioral interventions: Creating a structured routine, minimizing distractions, and using cues can help manage symptoms.
  • Cognitive stimulation: Engaging in cognitive activities can improve cognitive function and quality of life.
  • Caregiver support: Caregivers need support too, such as counseling, respite care, and support groups.
  • Safety measures: As the disease progresses, safety becomes a concern. Implementing safety measures such as removing tripping hazards and using monitoring devices can help.
