Mild Dementia

There is no cure for Alzheimer's disease or mild dementia caused by Alzheimer's, but treatments and strategies can manage symptoms and improve the quality of life of individuals with the condition.


Medications called "cholinesterase inhibitors" and "memantine" can help improve cognitive function and delay the progression of symptoms in some individuals with mild to moderate Alzheimer's disease.
Other strategies that may help individuals with mild dementia caused by Alzheimer's include:

  • Creating a structured routine for daily activities
  • Using memory aids such as calendars and notes
  • Simplifying tasks and breaking them into smaller steps
  • Providing a safe and comfortable environment
  • Engaging in activities that promote mental stimulation, such as puzzles or reading
